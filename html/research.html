Project Summary

As of Fall 2023, we are working to remove nalidixic acid resistance from Escherichia coli using CRISPR-Cas9 delivered via bacteriophage M13.

We performed mutagenesis on E. coli strand JM101 by selecting for nalidixic acid resistant colonies, and we are in the process of sequencing the genome of mutant colonies for our mutation of interest, this being gyrA codon 83 serine â†’ leucine.

We are also independently validating that the gyrA allele in our JM101 E. coli is wild-type using polymerase chain reaction to amplify the gene, and then sequencing it. This is being done to assure that the only gyrA mutation we are working with is the mutation of interest mentioed above.

Lastly, we have developed a protocol for inserting Cas9, green flourescent protein, a kanamycin resistance gene, and our custom sgRNA into bacteriophage M13. We intend to construct the modified phage and infect our resistant bacteria with it once the plasmid from which we shall isolate Cas9 ships from AddGene.
