Project Scope

Our current research plan is to cultivate colonies of antibiotic resistant neisseria gonorrhoeae, possibly along with other antibiotic resistant bacteria from other species, and to develop a CRISPR-Cas9 system capable of disarming these bacteria of their antibiotic resistance, or neutralizing them, once located in their cytoplasm. We shall then attempt to insert this CRISPR system into multiple, isolated bacterial colonies, using a separate delivery vector (such as bacteriophages, probiotics, nanoparticles, etc.) in each colony; the effectiveness of each delivery vector shall then be analyzed based on quantitative measurements of how many bacteria from each colony either perish or perish in the presence of antibiotics. We hope that this will serve as a step towards effective clinical treatment of antibiotic resistant infections by determining the most effective delivery vectors for such treatment.
